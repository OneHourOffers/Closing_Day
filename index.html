The Transaction Process
Step 01
Within 24 hours of contract acceptance

Contract sent to title

Within 24 hours of signing, your contract is delivered to a licensed title company or attorney. They open a file, assign an escrow officer, and begin the title search.

Step 02
7–14 days for title search

Title search & clearing

Title runs a search to confirm clear ownership and surface any liens, judgments, unpaid taxes, or claims against the property. If anything comes up, title works to clear it. You may be asked for documents — a death certificate, divorce decree, payoff statement — depending on what's found.

Step 03
As often as needed

Due diligence period

This is when our inspections and partner walkthroughs happen (see the Property Access section above). It's also when we finalize our financing/funding. If everything checks out, we move toward closing.

Step 04
Once we have clear to close

Closing Disclosures / Settlement Statement

Title prepares a settlement statement showing exactly how much you'll receive at closing — purchase price minus any payoffs (mortgage, liens, prorated taxes) and minus a small set of standard closing costs. You'll review and approve this before signing day.

Important reminder: 1 Hour Offers is not responsible for any mortgage(s), unpaid liens, judgments, or outstanding bills tied to the property. These remain your responsibility and are paid off out of your proceeds at closing.

Step 05
Signing typically 1–2 days before closing date

Signing & funding

You sign the deed and a few related documents — either at the title office, remotely with a mobile notary, or by mail. Once we've funded and the deed is recorded with the county, the title company is responsible for sending your proceeds based on how you've chosen to receive them — check, wire, etc.

Step 06
After Closing

Possession & handoff

Unless we've agreed to a post-closing occupancy, the property transfers to us at closing. We coordinate keys, garage remotes, and any final details. You're done — and we handle everything from here.

Common Questions You're probably wondering…
Do I need to be at closing?
Not necessarily. Most sellers sign remotely with a mobile notary or by overnighted documents. If you'd like to come to the title office in person, you're welcome to.
When do I get paid?
Once the deed is recorded with the county, the title company is responsible for sending your proceeds based on how you've chosen to receive them — check, wire, etc.
Who pays the closing costs?
That's spelled out in your contract. In most of our deals, we cover the standard closing costs (title insurance, recording fees, escrow). You're responsible for paying off any existing mortgage or liens, which comes out of your proceeds automatically.
What if I change my mind?
A signed contract is a binding agreement, so backing out has consequences depending on the terms. If something has changed in your life and you're worried, call us before doing anything else. We'd rather have a real conversation than a legal one.
What if the inspection finds problems?
We'll talk it over with you. Sometimes the price gets adjusted, sometimes we proceed as-is. We will never spring a renegotiation on you without first explaining what we found and why.
Can I leave things in the house?
Yes. Unlike a traditional buyer, we don't require the property to be empty or clean. Take what you want, leave what you don't — and we'll handle the rest.
